Which dataset did you choose for your classification problem?
I chose the Student Depression Dataset from Kaggle, which is designed for analysing and predicting depression among students.

What classes does it cover? Are they aligned with your prediction task?
It covers a binary depression label: depressed vs not depressed. This matches my prediction task, which is to classify whether a student should be considered in the depression class or not.

Why is this dataset ethically and technically fit for your project?
It is technically suitable because it is structured tabular data with relevant student-level features for classification. It is ethically suitable for an educational project because it addresses a meaningful mental health problem and can be used to study screening support rather than diagnosis.

What gaps remain, and how do you plan to address them?
Important gaps include self-report bias, uncertain representativeness, possible missing data, and the presence of highly target-proximal variables. I plan to address these by documenting limitations, checking data quality carefully, comparing feature sets, and keeping the intended use limited to human-reviewed screening support.
