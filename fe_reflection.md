Which engineered feature added the greatest predictive value, and why?
Strictly speaking, the notebook did not yet validate many truly new engineered interaction features. The largest measurable gain came from keeping the feature “Have you ever had suicidal thoughts ?” in the pipeline. When that variable was included, Logistic Regression reached about 0.918 ROC-AUC, compared with about 0.867 ROC-AUC without it, and Random Forest showed a similar drop when it was removed. This suggests that the strongest added predictive value came not from a new synthetic feature, but from retaining the most informative original mental-health-related feature.

What transformation had the most surprising impact?
The most surprising transformation was cleaning Financial Stress from object type to numeric after replacing "?" with missing values. At first, the dataset appeared to have no missing values at all, but after cleaning, hidden missingness was revealed. Another surprisingly important transformation was rare-category grouping, especially for Profession, which collapsed from 14 categories to essentially 2, showing that the feature had much less variation than expected.

Which step in your pipeline took the longest to debug, and what was the challenge?
The longest debugging step was the cross-validation section involving KNN. The notebook shows repeated warnings and NaN results caused by a threadpoolctl / environment-related error during scoring, rather than a conceptual modelling mistake. This made it difficult to tell whether the issue came from the data, the preprocessing, or the local Python environment. Compared with that, the preprocessing pipeline itself was much more stable.

How will your feature engineering choices affect model interpretability?
The current feature engineering choices support interpretability reasonably well. Numeric variables such as Academic Pressure, Financial Stress, Age, and Work/Study Hours remain easy to explain, and one-hot encoded categorical variables such as Dietary Habits and Sleep Duration can still be interpreted feature by feature. Grouping rare categories also improves interpretability by reducing unnecessary sparsity. However, keeping the suicidal-thoughts variable increases predictive power at the cost of making the model more ethically sensitive and more dependent on a target-proximal feature.

What would you do differently next time?
Next time, feature engineering would start earlier and more systematically. Instead of relying mainly on original variables, it would be better to test a small set of planned interaction features such as Academic Pressure × Financial Stress or Academic Pressure ÷ Study Satisfaction, and then validate them with mutual information or ablation tests. It would also be better to remove unstable models like KNN earlier once the environment issue became clear, and to separate clearly between preprocessing transformations and true engineered features so the contribution of each step is easier to evaluate.
